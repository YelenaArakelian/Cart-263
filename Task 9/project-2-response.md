Project 2 response

Reviewing the project “Honoring the legacy of Wowaka.”

by Ziyan Pan (Pansy) & Ben Woschitz

“Honoring the legacy of Wowaka” is an audio-visual tribute to Wowoka that uses Three.js to recreate the unique and minimal style for his covers. The project can be accessed here: https://pansyqwq.github.io/Cart263_Project/263-FinalFinal/

What stood out to me the most while going through the project was how interestingly the visuals were synchronized with the music playing. The use of very simple shapes, such as cubes and rectangles, makes up for the experience of this project, making it feel dynamic and engaging. Also, the minimalistic aesthetic worked effectively, as it creates a strong ambience without relying on complex and convoluted models or texture. In the track Tosenbo, the movements of the camera following the object’s rotation give a sense of immersion, almost as if the spectator is a part of the animation. Also, the overall design of the selection of the songs, the color palette, and the animation is very intriguing. The overall visual design of this project is very satisfying and successful.

From a technical point of view, this project’s use of Three.js was done interestingly. Rather than using realistic texture and complex lighting angles, this project kept everything flat and minimalist to match the original 2D style of Wowaka’s cover art. Also, easy access to the artist statement and the description of the project makes it more manageable and straightforward. All these utilities and designs show that they understood how to control the look and movement of their models using materials and lighting. Another thing that I found interesting is the way they connected the music to the animation, because it adds another layer to the experience and makes it feel more dynamic and engaging. I will assume that it took a lot of trials and errors to get it right.

Another thing I appreciate about this project is how the creators divided the work between them. Ben's tasks focused more on creating detailed models in Blender, while Pansy was in charge of working more with the animation of the code and with simple shapes. Like that, this balances not only the workload but also improves the performance because the project will run smoother compared to building everything manually.

To continue, something I found compelling to the project was how it uses simple and minimalist visuals to express a strong theme. The bouncing and rotating shapes illustrate a feeling of chaos and satisfaction, which fits perfectly with the themes of the playing music. Also, the tribute made to Wowoka warms my heart as an individual who used to enjoy some of his music.

Overall, I believe this work is a good exmaple of how such a simple and minimalist design can create an engaging and educational experience. It shows a good balance between coding and creativity, the end result is both visually interesting and meaningful.
